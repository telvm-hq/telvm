# Manufacturing communications arc — nine sessions (Puerto Rico lab narrative)

**Audience**: dinner guests + Luma list + optional social channels.  
**Principle**: **business and manufacturing transparency**, not health outcomes. Pair every public touch with the **disclaimer block** from [legal-copy-luma-and-venue.md](legal-copy-luma-and-venue.md).

**Series dates**: dinners are **weekly on Tuesdays** starting **April 28, 2026**; session 9 is **June 23, 2026**. Full table: [schedule.md](schedule.md).

---

## Session 1 — Why manufacturing matters

**Talking points**

- Moving from **prototype / concept** to **repeatable batches** requires **specifications** (identity, purity, strength for relevant categories), **documentation**, and **release testing**.
- Puerto Rico partner is being evaluated for **capacity**, **quality system**, and **logistics**—not “chosen forever” on day one.

**Deliverable**: one **one-pager PDF** (“What ‘manufacturing-ready’ means”) linked from Luma confirmation email.

---

## Session 2 — Specs, stability, and release testing (plain English)

**Talking points**

- **Stability** = how the product behaves over time (temperature, humidity, packaging).
- **Release testing** = tests run **before** a lot ships; what tests exist depends on **category and claims** (counsel-bound).

**Deliverable**: short **FAQ** post: “What we can/can’t say publicly.”

---

## Session 3 — Supplier qualification (Puerto Rico site)

**Talking points**

- **Audit / questionnaire** results: quality system, traceability, allergen handling if applicable.
- **Travel and samples**: what you physically saw vs what is still **paperwork in progress**.

**Risk line**: “Qualification can **pass**, **fail**, or require **re-work**.”

---

## Session 4 — Timeline risks (honest)

**Talking points**

- Raw material lead times, **equipment scheduling**, **analytical lab** queue times.
- **Hurricane season / logistics** as a generic operational risk (keep non-alarmist).

**Deliverable**: **timeline diagram** labeled **targets**, not promises.

---

## Session 5 — Formulation: excipients and shelf life

**Talking points**

- **Excipients** (fillers, binders, flavors) affect **flow, taste, and stability**.
- Changing one excipient can force **new stability studies**.

**Do not**: imply clinical benefit from formulation detail.

---

## Session 6 — Packaging, labeling, and lot tracking

**Talking points**

- **Child-resistant** needs if relevant; **lot codes** and **traceability**.
- **Label claims** are the narrowest legal surface—what appears on pack is **highly controlled**.

---

## Session 7 — Regulatory path (high-level only)

**Talking points**

- Category drives **FDA** expectations (e.g., supplement vs drug vs food—**counsel defines**).
- “**Not evaluated by FDA**” and similar language only where **legally accurate**.

**Mandatory**: counsel reviews this session’s script **word for word**.

---

## Session 8 — Pre-decision summary

**Talking points**

- Recap **open risks**: financing, MOQ, spec mismatches, third-party test backlog.
- “**Go**” means **specific contracts signed**; “**No-go**” means **pausing or pivoting**, not personal failure.

---

## Session 9 — Go / no-go rubric (publish before session 1 if possible)

Decision is **only** on **business milestones**, not on guest health feedback.

| Criterion | Weight | Evidence required | Go threshold |
|-----------|--------|-------------------|--------------|
| Quality system | High | Audit outcome / CAPA plan | Meets documented minimum |
| Spec agreement | High | Signed spec + test methods | Signed |
| COGS / MOQ | High | Quoted price at target volume | Fits fundraising or revenue model |
| Timeline | Medium | Gantt with dependencies | Critical path acceptable |
| Legal label path | High | Written regulatory opinion | No blocking issues |
| Insurance / liability | Medium | Policy endorsement if needed | Bound |

**Outcomes**

- **Go**: announce **next concrete step** (e.g., “execute master service agreement,” “first engineering run”).
- **No-go**: announce **what pauses** and **what options remain** (different site, different format, timeline slip).

**Guest-facing line (session 9 close)**

“Thank you for nine weeks of support. Our **decision today** is **[Go / No-go]** based on **[criteria above in plain language]**. This **does not** change medical guidance for celiac disease—we’re grateful you shared the table with us.”

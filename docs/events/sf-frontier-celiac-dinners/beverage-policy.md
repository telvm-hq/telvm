# Beverage policy — wine + beer (celiac-safe series)

## Policy summary

- **Wine**: included in ticket; target **~5 standard pours per guest** (~**1 bottle equivalent** per person per night).
- **Beer**: **only beers that are labeled or certified gluten-free** from **non-barley** or explicitly **celiac-accepted** positioning your counsel approves. **Do not position standard Corona Extra (barley malt) as celiac-safe.** If a sponsor insists on “Corona family,” map to **Corona-branded products that are actually GF** (e.g., seltzers or other SKUs your counsel verifies for **your** claims and ABC treatment)—never assume “Corona = GF” for celiac marketing.

## Standard pour math (lock this for budgeting and training)

Assume **750 ml bottle**, US “glass” often modeled at **5 oz (~148 ml)** per pour.

| Pours per guest | ml per guest | Bottles per 30 guests (750 ml) |
|-----------------|-------------|--------------------------------|
| 5 | ~740 | **~30 bottles** |
| 4 | ~592 | **~24 bottles** |

**Recommendation**: publish **5 paired pours** on the menu (e.g., “Pour 1 with apps … Pour 5 with dessert”) so guests understand pacing and you control variance.

### Wholesale budget anchor (~$100 all-in ticket)

If wine is ~30 bottles × **$10** = **~$300** COGS wine only; scale food and labor accordingly or reduce to **4 pours** / slightly smaller pours. Document the chosen line in the event P&L.

## Beer service levels (pick one; document on Luma)

| Tier | Allocation | Notes |
|------|------------|--------|
| A | **2 × 12 oz certified GF cans** per guest | Safe default for celiac positioning |
| B | **1 can + cash bar** for additional certified GF | Lowers ticket pressure |
| C | **GF beer flight** (3 × 4 oz) | Tasting format; train staff on pour sizes |

**Brand strategy**: rotate **2–3 certified GF breweries** per night (import + domestic) for variety; keep **ingredient statements** on hand for questions.

## Gluten-removed beer (optional)

Some products are “gluten removed” from barley. **Many celiac diners avoid them.** If you serve them at all:

- **Separate signage** from “certified GF / non-barley.”
- **Legal + medical counsel** approval before any disease-adjacent copy.

## ABC and ticket structure (California — verify with counsel)

Questions for counsel:

1. Is wine/beer **included in the ticket price** treated as acceptable under your service model and venue license?
2. If a sponsor donates product, does that change **ABC** or **FTC disclosure** obligations on Luma?
3. **Age verification** at entry; **service refusal** policy documented for staff.

## Staff training (one-pager)

- Measure or demo **5 oz** pour; show **cutoff** after fifth pour unless manager override.
- **No double-fisting** with beer + wine during hot service if it risks safety; optional soft rule.
- **Water** readily available (celiac community often appreciates aggressive hydration messaging without supplement claims).
